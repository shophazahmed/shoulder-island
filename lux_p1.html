3-Year Roadmap
Months 1–3 — Admin & Lease
Secure Island & Planning
Apply for uninhabited island lease via Ministry of Agriculture / Ministry of Economy. Commission site survey, EIA, and master plan. Register company and apply for import duty exemptions. Sign MOU with 2+ resort buyers. Apply for relevant licences across all ventures in parallel.
Months 3–6 — Build
Infrastructure Construction
Install solar microgrid + battery storage. Build poultry sheds, hydroponic polytunnels, cold room, staff quarters, and initial jetty. Establish water supply (rainwater + RO). Construct composting bays and BSF module.
Months 3–4 — Operations Start
Transfer Hydroponics + Start Coconut
Transfer hydroponic equipment and stock from existing island. Begin first planting cycle. Start coconut harvest and VCO pressing. First resort supply deliveries by Month 4.
Months 4–8 — Poultry Live
First Poultry Harvest
Import Day-Old Chicks and point-of-lay pullets. First egg collection Month 5. First broiler harvest and resort delivery Month 7. Sign first B2B supply agreements with resorts.
Months 9–12 — Lagoon Launch
Seaweed + Sea Cucumber + Tourism
Deploy Kappaphycus longlines in lagoon. Sea cucumber hatchery commissioned; first spawning cycle begins. First seaweed harvest at Month 11. Launch eco-tourism day trips. Apply for Verra carbon credit registration.
Year 2 — Scale Up
Full Operations & Aquaculture Growth
Scale poultry to 10,000 broilers + 5,000 layers. Sea cucumber juveniles transferred to lagoon grow-out pens. Add IMTA lobster/shrimp. Expand eco-tourism to 5 days/week. Expand hydroponic area to 1,500 m².
Months 20–24 — First Export
Sea Cucumber Export Revenue Begins
Sea cucumbers reach 350–425g harvest size. First commercial processing: evisceration, boiling, smoking, drying to beche-de-mer. First export shipment to Chinese buyer. Second hatchery cycle begins immediately.
Year 3+ — Full Scale
Export + Carbon + Expansion
Sea cucumber at 7+ MT/year dried → MVR 15.4M+ export, scaling toward MVR 129.4M. All ventures at design capacity. Receive first Verra carbon credit payments. Explore pearl culture and coral nursery grants.
Circular Economy
🔗 Every venture feeds the others — reducing costs, eliminating waste, and multiplying total revenue from the same inputs.
🐔 Poultry ↔ 🌿 Hydroponics
→Hydroponic waste (stems, roots, unsold greens) = free poultry feed. Saves 10–15% on feed costs annually
→Poultry manure composted → premium grow media for hydroponics. Closes the nutrient loop
→Black Soldier Fly larvae (fed on farm food waste) → high-protein chicken feed. Cuts feed bill 20–30%
🐟 Sea Cucumber ↔ 🌊 Seaweed (IMTA)
→Seaweed absorbs nitrogen from fish/shrimp waste — acts as natural bio-filter. Zero chemical treatment
→Sea cucumbers feed on organic detritus settling below seaweed lines — cleaning the lagoon floor passively
→IMTA system increases revenue per hectare 200–400% vs single-species
☀️ Solar ↔ Cold Chain ↔ All Ventures
→Solar powers cold room for chicken, eggs, dried sea cucumber, VCO — preserving premium quality for export
→All organic waste → bagged compost → sold to resort gardens. Near-zero extra cost, pure revenue
→Coconut shells → biochar → mixed into compost for premium "terra preta" product at 30% price premium
🤿 Eco Tourism ↔ All Ventures
→Resort guests tour the farm → resort F&B managers sign supply contracts for eggs, chicken, herbs, VCO
→Day-trip income covers island operating costs in Years 1–2 while aquaculture is scaling up
→Coral nursery at jetty → conservation story → qualifies for GFCR grant funding
🥥 Coconut ↔ Everything
→Palms already growing — almost zero raw material cost. Every product = near-100% margin on raw material
→Coconut water → poultry electrolyte supplement. Coir husks replace imported coco peat in hydroponics
